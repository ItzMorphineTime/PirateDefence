# The Shattered Seas — World Lore & Backstory

> *"The old empires drowned. The seas broke. Five crowns rose from the wreckage—and none of them bow to any king on land."*

---

## The World: The Shattered Seas

The **Shattered Seas** are what remains of a once-balanced world. To most sailors, the truth of its ruin is lost beneath rumour, superstition, propaganda, and salt.

Centuries ago, a cataclysm—some say a war between gods, others a volcanic chain reaction, still others whisper of something that *woke* beneath the waves—shattered the continental shelves and drowned the great coastal empires. What survived was an archipelago of scattered islands, treacherous straits, haunted routes, and waters that no longer obey the old charts.

Today, the seas are ruled by **trade, rumour, and fear**. The inner archipelago—the **Home Waters**—remains relatively safe: established ports, merchant guilds, lighthouse keepers, and the last vestiges of naval authority. But beyond the safe routes lie the **Outer Seas**, where the **Five Pirate Kings** hold dominion. Each King commands a domain shaped by their curse, their ambition, their trauma, or their obsession. Sailors who venture too far may never return—or return changed.

The world did not become this way in a single moment. Its history is remembered in ages:

| Age | Common Name | Dominant Power | What It Means |
|-----|-------------|----------------|---------------|
| **First Age** | The Age of Three Crowns | Humans, Atlanteans, Dragons | Balance between Land, Sea, and Sky |
| **Cataclysm** | The Shattering / The Drowning | None | The Crown Below breaks the old world |
| **Second Age** | The Age of the Drowned Crown | Atlanteans | Atlantis dominates the flooded world |
| **Collapse Event** | The Unbinding Tide | Sea Serpents, Krakens, Deep Beasts | The deep rebels against Atlantean rule |
| **Third Age** | The Age of Free Sails | Pirates, Merchants, Free Captains | Trade returns and pirate glory rises |
| **Current Age** | The Age of Five Crowns / Broken Tides | The Pirate Kings | Five powers divide the seas while dragons near extinction |

The player enters the Shattered Seas hearing only fragments:

- That the gods fought and cracked the world.
- That a chain of volcanoes erupted and swallowed the continents.
- That dragons burned the skies and fled.
- That Atlantis tried to save the world and was betrayed.
- That pirates dug too greedily into forbidden places.
- That something ancient woke beneath the waves.
- That the ghost captain Jasper Barrow caused it all.
- That Ebon Flameheart once saved trade and gave glory back to pirates.

All of these stories contain a shard of truth.

None of them are the whole truth.

## Story / Lore: The Sundering of the Three Crowns

> *Copied from Tidewarden fragments, Atlantean mural translations, surviving Admiralty logs, dragon sanctuary oral histories, and the testimony of sailors who claim to have seen green-gold fire in the fog.*

Before the Shattered Seas, there was a golden age remembered only in fragments: **The First Age of the Three Crowns**.

The "crowns" were not merely royal titles. They were sacred charges: three realms, three peoples, and three duties that kept the world whole.

### The Crown of Land

The humans of the great continents built cities, farms, harbours, roads, libraries, and navies. They were mortal, ambitious, inventive, and numerous. They did not command the oldest magic, but they had memory, law, craft, courage, and the will to cross any horizon.

Their charge was **stewardship of the land**.

They built the lighthouse chains, maintained the ports, grew the food, forged the cannons, and charted the safe roads between mountain, jungle, desert, and coast. Their admiralties guarded trade routes and kept sacred charts that marked not only reefs and storms, but the hidden places where dragons nested.

### The Crown of Sea

The Atlanteans ruled beneath the waves. They were not merely humans who lived underwater, but an ancient ocean-born people adapted to pressure, current, song, and tide. They built coral palaces, pearl engines, glass domes, leviathan gates, tide-forges, and libraries sung into living shell.

Their charge was **stewardship of the sea**.

Atlanteans could not live long upon dry land. Their skin dried, their lungs burned, and their magic weakened away from saltwater. For this reason, they relied on treaties, surface envoys, and human navigators to understand the continents above. They knew the deep better than any surface kingdom, but the mountains, volcanoes, high cliffs, and inland dragon rookeries were beyond their reach.

### The Crown of Sky

The dragons ruled neither as kings nor conquerors. They were ancient sovereign creatures who nested in volcanoes, storm peaks, cliff-rookeries, floating isles, jungle calderas, frozen ridges, and hidden ruins. Their migrations shaped winds. Their songs steadied storms. Their eggs carried old elemental memory.

Their charge was **stewardship of the sky**.

The dragons did not own the world. They anchored it.

Where dragons nested, the world was stable. Volcanoes slept. Storms followed seasons. Oceans respected their shores. Magic flowed in cycles rather than floods. Each clutch was more than a generation of dragons; it was a promise between land, sea, and sky.

### The First Age

In the First Age, humanity, Atlantis, and dragonkind were not always friends, but they understood balance.

Humans traded surface goods to Atlantis: timber, iron, fruit, spices, cloth, stories, and maps. Atlanteans traded sea-gifts to humans: pearls, medicines, current-charts, coral glass, tide charms, and leviathan-safe passages. Dragons rarely traded, but they gave warnings. A dragon circling a harbour might mean storm. A dragon vanishing from a mountain might mean eruption. A clutch abandoned before hatching was considered a prophecy of disaster.

This age was prosperous because no one power could rule all three realms.

Humans could not command the deep.

Atlanteans could not walk the land.

Dragons would not kneel to either.

That limitation was the source of peace.

Then Atlantis grew ashamed of needing others.

## The Atlantean Hunger

As the centuries passed, the Atlantean high houses became obsessed with a forbidden question:

**Why should the sea merely be one crown of three?**

Their empire was vast, beautiful, ancient, and powerful—but trapped beneath the surface. They watched human kingdoms multiply across the land. They watched dragons soar over both land and sea without borders. To the most ambitious Atlanteans, balance began to look like humiliation.

The ruling faction that embraced this belief became known as **The Pearl Synod**. They argued that Atlantis had been denied its rightful destiny. They believed only the sea was eternal, and that all land, sky, wind, fire, beast, and kingdom should eventually answer to the tide.

To achieve this, the Pearl Synod designed a ritual engine beneath the Atlantean capital:

**The Crown Below.**

The Crown Below was not simply an artifact. It was a world-commanding device. It was intended to bind land, sea, and sky into one throne of tidal law.

To awaken it, the Synod needed three kinds of power:

1. **The blood-memory of dragons** — contained most purely in their eggs.
2. **The obedience of leviathans** — controlled through Atlantean tide-song.
3. **The maps of the surface world** — especially the hidden locations of dragon nests.

Atlantis already had the leviathans.

They lacked the maps.

So they turned to a man of the surface.

---

## Admiral Barrow's Betrayal

**Admiral Cassian Barrow**, father of Jasper Barrow, was one of the most trusted men in the old Imperial Admiralty. He was not only a commander, but the **Lord-Cartographer of the Admiralty**, keeper of its most sacred and forbidden charts.

Those charts marked more than trade routes. They recorded storm passages, hidden islands, dragon migration paths, rookery cliffs, volcanic nests, old treaty markers, and the sanctuaries where the dragons of the First Age raised their young.

The Atlanteans could not reach those places themselves. They could not climb the volcanic ridges where fire dragons nested. They could not cross the inland calderas where ancient clutches slept. They could not scale the storm cliffs where wind dragons brooded.

So the Pearl Synod offered Cassian Barrow what every proud traitor believes he deserves:

Power.

Wealth.

Immortality.

A throne after the ritual.

Cassian was promised rule over the surface fleets once Atlantis rose above all other powers. He would become the first human Lord-Admiral of the New Tide Empire.

Cassian accepted.

He did not raid the nests himself. That would expose him. Instead, he released copied charts to pirate lords, privateers, smugglers, disgraced captains, and treasure fleets hungry for impossible wealth. Some were told the dragon eggs were priceless relics. Some were promised gold. Some were promised pardons. Some were promised Atlantean weapons. Some were simply told that dragon eggs could make men into kings.

This was the original sin of the pirate age:

**the first great dragon hunts were not random acts of piracy, but a conspiracy between Atlantis, a corrupt admiral, and the pirate lords of the surface.**

The Pirate Kings of the present are heirs to that sin, whether they know it or not.

---

## Jasper Barrow and the False Treason

Jasper Barrow was raised in the shadow of his father's honour. He believed the Admiralty protected the innocent. He believed charts were sacred because they kept sailors alive. He believed power existed to defend those without it.

That is why he became dangerous.

As a young captain, Jasper began noticing impossible patterns:

- Dragon nests marked as "extinct" shortly before pirate raids.
- Pirate vessels avoiding naval patrols with impossible accuracy.
- Atlantean pearl coin found in the hands of surface raiders.
- Refugees speaking of eggs taken alive, not treasure stolen.
- His father quietly altering Admiralty records after each attack.

At first, Jasper thought there was a spy inside the Admiralty.

Then he found the duplicate charts.

Not stolen.

Signed.

Sealed.

Released under his father's authority.

Jasper confronted Cassian in private, still hoping there was an explanation. Cassian did not deny it. He told Jasper that the First Age was ending. That dragons were relics. That humans were weak because they clung to old treaties. That Atlantis had the will to remake the world, and that the Barrow name would stand at the right hand of the new empire.

Jasper refused.

He threatened to expose him.

So Cassian destroyed his son.

### The False Distress Call

Cassian arranged for Jasper to receive a distress signal from a remote storm route near a dragon migration path. The message claimed that a civilian convoy had been trapped by pirates.

Jasper sailed immediately.

But there was no convoy.

Only pirate lords waiting in the storm, carrying Barrow charts, Atlantean shot, and orders to leave no survivors.

Jasper's ship, **The Righteous Tide**, fought until its masts burned and its hull split. His crew died believing they had been abandoned by the Admiralty. Jasper was wounded, chained to the wheel, and left to sink beneath black water.

Cassian declared Jasper a traitor.

The official story became that Jasper had sold Admiralty charts to pirates, murdered his own crew, and fled into the storm. That lie survived the cataclysm and still stains his name in some ports.

Some sailors still curse Jasper Barrow as the first traitor-captain.

Others whisper that he was the only honest man in the Admiralty.

---

## Aurelion's Dive

As Jasper sank, an Elder Dragon was nearby.

His name was **Aurelion Veyr**, the Last Dawn-Wing.

Aurelion was one of the oldest dragons of the First Age, guardian of a hidden migration route and keeper of several scattered clutches. He had already sensed the theft of eggs across the world. He had felt nests go silent. He had heard unborn dragon-song vanish.

Then he felt something different.

Not the fear of prey.

Not the rage of battle.

A betrayal so deep it echoed like a wound in the world.

Jasper's soul, still burning with grief and justice, called through the storm.

Aurelion dived from the clouds into the sea.

Sailor myth remembers this only as a flash of gold beneath black waves: a dragon-shaped dawn vanishing into the storm.

Aurelion could not save Jasper's life.

But he saved his soul.

He carried Jasper's dying spirit into the deep, away from the pirates, away from Cassian's lie, away from the reach of Atlantis. There, beneath the storm, dragonfire met seawater and created something impossible:

**Veilflame.**

Not living fire.

Not ghost magic.

A sacred flame that burns only around broken oaths, betrayed innocents, and buried truths.

---

## The Crown Below Ritual

By then, the Atlanteans had gathered enough eggs.

The pirate lords delivered crates of them into the deep: fire eggs, storm eggs, reef eggs, moon eggs, frost eggs, ash eggs, and ancient clutches stolen from nests older than human kingdoms.

The Pearl Synod placed them around the Crown Below. They believed dragon eggs were batteries: sources of sky-magic to be cracked, drained, and commanded.

They were wrong.

Dragon eggs were not containers of power.

They were promises.

Each egg was bound to a place, a season, a current, a mountain, a wind, a future migration, and a balance not yet born.

When the ritual began, the Crown Below tried to pull all three realms into one command.

It reached for the land through stolen maps.

It reached for the sea through leviathan song.

It reached for the sky through unborn dragons.

For one heartbeat, it worked.

The tides stopped.

The stars bent downward.

Every dragon in the world screamed.

Then the unborn refused.

The eggs broke, but they did not obey.

Instead of crowning Atlantis, the ritual tore the covenants of the First Age apart.

The continental shelves shattered.

Atlantis sank deeper than the sea was meant to go.

Volcanic chains erupted.

Storms became permanent.

Dragon routes vanished.

Leviathans went mad.

Old gods, sea beasts, or abyssal things—depending on who tells the tale—stirred beneath the wound.

The world became the Shattered Seas.

This event is remembered by many names:

- **The Shattering**
- **The Drowning**
- **The Sundering of the Three Crowns**
- **The Night the Sea Rose**
- **The Breaking of the First Age**

---

## Jasper's Binding

During the catastrophe, Aurelion knew the world had been wounded beyond simple healing.

The dragons were scattered.

Atlantis was drowning.

Human kingdoms were collapsing.

The seas were filling with pirates, monsters, wrecks, and lies.

Jasper's body was dead. His name had been ruined. His crew was gone. But his soul remained fiercely intact.

Aurelion offered him a choice.

Not resurrection.

Not peace.

A charge.

Jasper could pass beyond death and leave the world to its ruin. Or he could be bound to the sea as a revenant oath: a guardian of the betrayed, a hunter of tyrants, and a witness against the lie that destroyed the First Age.

Jasper accepted.

Aurelion bound Jasper's soul to the Veilwake Sea with dragonfire, seawater, and the last breath of the storm.

From that moment, Jasper Barrow became:

**The Ghost Captain of the Righteous Tide.**  
**The First Drowned Oath.**  
**The Witness in the Fog.**  
**The Man the Sea Would Not Let Die.**

Jasper's undeath is not merely a curse. It is a sacred wound. He is consecrated by betrayal and chained to justice until corruption, tyranny, and the crimes against dragonkind are answered.

### The Fate of Aurelion Veyr

Aurelion vanished after binding Jasper.

Some say he died and became the golden dawn sometimes seen over the Veilwake Sea. Others claim his bones lie wrapped around the first dragon sanctuary. Some Tidewardens believe his body still sleeps beneath **Dawnrest**, the oldest hidden sanctuary, and that his dreams guide lost eggs home.

The truth of Aurelion's fate is one of the deepest mysteries of the Shattered Seas.

---

## The Crown Below: The Wound That Still Pulses

The Crown Below did not vanish after the ritual failed.

It remains beneath the deepest ruins of Atlantis: broken, buried, and leaking power.

It no longer has enough strength to command land, sea, and sky, but it continues to distort the world. Its pulse explains why the Shattered Seas are still unstable, why old routes change, why magic returns in dangerous forms, why sea monsters stir, and why the domains of the Pirate Kings are warped by supernatural influence.

The Crown Below had two consequences.

The first was immediate: **The Shattering** broke the old world.

The second was slower and stranger: **the deep learned to rebel**.

The ritual corrupted the ancient bonds by which Atlanteans had bargained with leviathans, guided great sea serpents away from cities, and contained krakens in abyssal trenches. After the Crown Below failed, those bonds did not simply snap. They rotted. The things bound by them became stronger, more intelligent, more wrathful, and less obedient.

Every stolen dragon egg, every slain dragon, every awakened leviathan, every serpent curse, every abyssal song, and every act of tyrannical conquest risks feeding the old wound.

The greatest danger is not that the world was broken once.

It is that the world can be broken again.

## The Second Age: The Age of the Drowned Crown

> *"When the land drowned, those who breathed water called themselves merciful."*  
> — Tidewarden marginal note, recovered from Lantern Cay

The Shattering ended the First Age, but it did not end Atlantean ambition.

In the immediate aftermath of the cataclysm, the surface world was broken. Continents had fractured into island chains. Coastal capitals lay beneath silt and salt. Human navies were shattered. Lighthouse chains went dark. Roads ended at cliffs where the sea had swallowed provinces whole. Dragons vanished into grief, hiding, or extinction.

Atlantis had also suffered terribly, but the world had become more ocean than land—and that made the Atlanteans the strongest surviving power.

Thus began the **Second Age**, remembered by Atlantean loyalists as **The Age of Restoration**, by surface ports as **The Age of Tribute**, and by most sailors as:

**The Age of the Drowned Crown.**

During this age, Atlantis dominated the flooded world. Atlantean houses claimed that only they could make the new seas navigable. They rebuilt current-roads, raised coral toll-gates, installed pearl-tax collectors in surviving harbours, and forced surface ports into tribute pacts in exchange for safe passage.

They built:

- **Tide gates** that redirected dangerous currents.
- **Coral citadels** around drowned ruins.
- **Leviathan roads** guarded by sea beasts.
- **Pearl-tax harbours** that controlled surviving trade.
- **Underwater prisons** for rebels, smugglers, and dragon sympathizers.
- **Drowned tollhouses** along routes that had once been free.
- **Current engines** powered by fragments of the Crown Below.

To many starving islands, Atlantean rule was better than chaos.

To free captains, it was conquest with polished manners.

To the dragons, it was the same old hunger wearing a crown of coral.

### Adara's Inheritance

Lady Adara Thalassa was born long after the Second Age's height, but she was raised on its memory. To her, the Age of the Drowned Crown was not tyranny. It was proof that Atlantis could impose order on a broken world.

When Adara speaks of restoring Atlantis, she does not merely mean rebuilding coral palaces.

She means restoring the authority of the Drowned Crown over the seas.

She means tribute ports.

She means obedient leviathans.

She means dragons brought to heel.

She means a world where no route exists outside Atlantean law.

---

## The Corruption of the Deep

The Atlanteans believed the deep belonged to them.

They were wrong.

Before the Crown Below, Atlantean tide-song could bargain with leviathans, turn serpent migrations away from cities, and lull krakens into abyssal sleep. Those songs were not absolute control; they were ancient agreements, layered over centuries of ritual, respect, fear, and mutual need.

The Crown Below corrupted those agreements.

Its failed command taught the deep a terrible lesson: that all bindings could be broken.

Sea serpents grew larger and more cunning. Krakens woke from trenches where they had slept since before Atlantis. Leviathans became harder to calm. Lesser sea beasts began following strange currents toward the broken pulse beneath the world.

Atlantean scholars called it **resonant instability**.

Surface sailors called it **the sea getting teeth**.

Tidewardens called it the second consequence of the Pearl Synod's sin.

> *The Crown Below did not simply fail. It taught the deep how to rebel.*

---

## The Unbinding Tide

The event that ended the Second Age is remembered by many names:

- **The Unbinding Tide**
- **The Breaking of the Leash**
- **The War of Unbound Depths**
- **The Uncoiling**
- **The Night the Coral Roads Fell**

The most common name is **The Unbinding Tide**.

It began when the sea serpents stopped avoiding Atlantean cities.

Then the krakens rose.

No single king, beast, or battle caused the collapse. It was a season of disasters. Serpents coiled around tide gates and crushed them. Kraken arms pulled coral bridges into the abyss. Leviathans refused commands and turned on their handlers. Current engines overloaded. Pearl-tax harbours lost contact with the deep roads. Underwater prisons cracked open. Entire Atlantean districts were dragged into trenches that had no names.

The Atlanteans had built an empire on the belief that the sea could be commanded.

The sea answered.

The Unbinding Tide devastated Atlantean infrastructure so completely that surface ports began to breathe again. Trade routes that had been taxed by Atlantis became dangerous but open. Smugglers became merchants. Privateers became protectors. Pirates became heroes in their own songs.

The Second Age ended not because humans defeated Atlantis, but because the monsters of the deep broke the empire first.

This collapse made the Third Age possible.

### The Legacy of the Unbinding Tide

The Unbinding Tide did not make the world safe.

It made the world available.

For the first time since the Shattering, independent captains could cross routes without bowing to Atlantean tolls. They still had to face serpents, krakens, storms, pirates, and ruined magic—but those dangers could be fought, avoided, bargained with, or outrun.

This is the age when free captains became more than survivors.

They became powers.

---

## The Third Age: The Age of Free Sails

> *"Ebon gave men teeth enough to bite monsters."*  
> — Old pirate toast, still heard in Ashen Reach taverns

After the Unbinding Tide, the world entered the **Third Age**.

Pirates call it **The Age of Free Sails**.

Merchants call it **The Route-Reopening**.

Tidewardens and dragon sanctuary keepers have a darker name for it:

**The Ashen Prosperity.**

This was the age when humans and pirates rose in the power vacuum left by Atlantis. Trade returned. Ports reopened. Convoys sailed again. Free captains mapped routes through monster waters, salvaged Atlantean ruins, raided old tollhouses, and learned how to survive the new sea.

But prosperity came at a cost.

The Third Age was also when dragon-hunting became a craft.

Before the Age of Free Sails, stealing a dragon egg was a desperate act, a miracle, or a death sentence. After it, there were methods. There were tools. There were markets. There were buyers.

And one captain stood at the centre of that transformation.

**Ebon Flameheart.**

---

## Ebon's Glory and the Ashen Turn

Ebon Flameheart did not begin as the monster he is now.

In the early Third Age, he was celebrated as one of the greatest free captains ever to sail. He broke serpent blockades, charted ash currents, raided Atlantean ruin-vaults, rescued merchant convoys from kraken waters, and taught pirates how to survive where old navies feared to go.

Pirates still sing that Ebon gave glory back to men.

There is truth in that.

He taught free captains:

- how to read monster wakes before a serpent surfaced,
- how to evade kraken feeding grounds,
- how to use volcanic vents and ash winds,
- how to raid Atlantean wreck-cities,
- how to reinforce hulls against fire and acid,
- how to build harpoons from dragonbone and leviathan steel,
- how to hunt dragons by dawn-smoke, compass failure, and nesting heat,
- how to turn scales, bone, claws, and eggs into weapons, armour, and coin.

To pirates and merchants, Ebon was a liberator.

To dragons, he was the beginning of an industry.

Cassian Barrow sold the first maps.

Ebon Flameheart taught the world how to use them.

### Blackfang

During one of his most famous raids on a volcanic rookery, Ebon found a single surviving dragon egg among ash, glass, and broken shell. Stories disagree about why he kept it.

Some say greed.

Some say pity.

Some say the egg cracked in his hands and the hatchling looked at him like a storm looks at a mast.

Whatever the truth, Ebon did not sell the egg.

He hatched it.

He named the dragon **Blackfang**.

For a time, Ebon and Blackfang were not master and weapon, but companions. Ebon raised the hatchling aboard his ship. Blackfang learned cannon thunder before mountain wind. He slept around warm anchors, stole fish from barrels, snapped at crewmen who mocked him, and grew into a terror of the skies with a pirate captain as his chosen friend.

Their bond changed Ebon.

Blackfang's dragonfire spirit entered his blood. Ebon's wounds began to cauterise with ember-light. Fire refused to consume him completely. His age slowed. His rage strengthened Blackfang, and Blackfang's fury strengthened him.

Ebon became nearly immortal.

Not because he conquered a dragon.

Because, once, a dragon chose him.

### The Ashen Turn

That truth became Ebon's deepest fear.

If Blackfang had chosen him, another dragon might choose another captain.

Another pirate could gain dragonfire spirit.

Another crew could become legend.

Another Dragon King could rise.

Ebon's glory curdled into paranoia. The captain who once taught pirates how to survive the monstrous world began burning dragon nests to prevent anyone else from receiving the gift he had received. He paid bounties for eggs not to hatch them, but to crush them. He slaughtered migrating dragons. He destroyed rookeries he had once spared. He turned the techniques of the Age of Free Sails into a machine of extinction.

Thus ended the bright myth of Ebon Flameheart.

The liberator became the hoarder.

The teacher became the tyrant.

The friend of Blackfang became the Dragon Marauder.

---

## The Current Age: The Age of Five Crowns

The current age is called many things.

Merchants call it **The Age of Broken Tides**.

Pirates call it **The Age of Five Crowns**.

Tidewardens call it **The Last Chance**.

The Atlantean empire is broken, but not gone. Dragonkind is nearly extinct, but not dead. The free ports endure, but only by trade, courage, compromise, and luck. Pirate prosperity has soured into piracy, strongholds, tribute, monster cults, dragon bounties, and kings who answer to no law but their own.

The Five Pirate Kings are the children of every age before them:

| King | Historical Wound |
|------|------------------|
| **Jasper Barrow** | The betrayal that caused the Shattering |
| **Adara Thalassa** | The failed empire of the Drowned Crown |
| **Mordekai Drakon** | The human trauma of Atlantean domination and serpent corruption |
| **Nimue Tideborn** | The freedom and horror of the Unbinding Tide |
| **Ebon Flameheart** | The pirate prosperity that became dragon genocide |

The player enters now, when every old sin is still alive.

The Crown Below still pulses.

The deep is still unbound.

Ebon still hunts dragons.

Adara still dreams of Atlantean order.

Mordekai still carries serpent hatred in his blood.

Nimue still listens to the abyss.

Jasper still judges the fog.

And somewhere, hidden in cargo holds, rookery ruins, sanctuary coves, and the dreams of Aurelion Veyr, the last dragon eggs wait to be saved.

## Public Rumours vs. Hidden Truth

Most people do not know the true history of the Shattered Seas. The player should uncover it gradually through quests, wrecks, charts, murals, ghostly encounters, sanctuary lore, and conflicting faction testimony.

### Common Sailor Rumours

- "The Shattering was a war between sea gods."
- "The dragons burned the world and fled."
- "Atlantis tried to save us, but the surface kingdoms betrayed them."
- "Pirates cracked the world open looking for gold."
- "Something woke beneath the waves and swallowed the continents."
- "Jasper Barrow caused it. That's why his ghost still sails."
- "The Unbinding Tide was when the sea monsters took revenge for being chained."
- "Ebon Flameheart gave the sea back to men."

### The Atlantean Version

Atlantean loyalists blame the surface world. They say humans broke the old treaties, pirates slaughtered dragons carelessly, and Atlantis attempted a desperate ritual to restore order before the world collapsed.

They describe the Age of the Drowned Crown as a mercy: a necessary order imposed on a broken world.

They describe the Unbinding Tide as barbarism: beasts and surface scavengers destroying the last true civilisation.

Some Atlanteans know this is a lie.

Others believe it completely.

### The Admiralty Version

The remaining human authorities blame pirates, dragons, natural disaster, and old sea magic. They bury Cassian Barrow's role because admitting that the Lord-Cartographer of the Admiralty helped cause the apocalypse would destroy what little faith remains in naval law.

Officially, Jasper Barrow remains a traitor in some Admiralty archives.

Unofficially, many officers know the ink on those records smells like rot.

### The Pirate Version

Pirates tell stories of three glories.

The first is the **Egg Rush**, when dragon eggs were worth more than kingdoms.

The second is the **Unbinding Tide**, when Atlantis lost its grip and the sea became open to those brave enough to take it.

The third is the **Age of Free Sails**, when Ebon Flameheart taught pirates to fight monsters, reopen routes, and grow rich on the bones of empires.

Most pirates do not see these as sins.

They see them as proof that fortune belongs to those bold enough to steal it.

### The Tidewarden Version

The Tidewardens preserve the hidden truth: the world was wounded by greed, and every age since has been shaped by those who tried to profit from the wound.

They believe dragon rescue is not charity. It is repair.

Every egg saved is a promise restored.

Every sanctuary protected is a stitch in the torn covenant of Land, Sea, and Sky.

### Jasper's Version

Jasper remembers the truth, but not cleanly.

His memory is fractured by death, dragonfire, grief, and centuries of vengeance. When the player first meets him, he speaks in accusations, warnings, and half-remembered sorrow. Only by recovering relics, charts, bells, eggshell fragments, and ship logs can the player help him remember the whole story.

Jasper's judgment of Ebon is especially bitter:

> "My father sold the sky. Ebon taught men how to butcher it."

## Dragons: The Last of the Great Beasts

**Dragons** once ruled the skies and volcanic isles of the Shattered Seas—ancient, proud, and few. In the First Age, they were not merely powerful creatures. They were living anchors of the world.

Where dragons nested, the world held steady. Their eggs carried elemental memory. Their migrations shaped wind, tide, storm, and volcanic pressure. Their songs warned of imbalance. Their absence is one reason the seas remain broken.

The cataclysm scattered their nesting grounds, destroyed countless clutches, and drove the survivors to the edges of the world. The Age of Free Sails turned their persecution into an industry. Now they are **nearly extinct**, hunted to the brink by those who covet their power.

### Why Dragons Matter

Dragons are not only rare beasts or powerful allies. They are part of the world's structure.

- **Volcanic dragons** calm eruptions and regulate fire beneath the islands.
- **Wind dragons** guide storms, trade winds, and migration paths.
- **Frost dragons** steady northern currents and ice routes.
- **Reef dragons** protect coral gates, island shelves, and hidden coves.
- **Elder dragons** remember the covenants of the First Age.

Every dragon killed makes the world less stable.

Every egg saved is a small repair to the broken covenant of land, sea, and sky.

### Why the Kings Hunt Dragons

- **Dragon eggs** can be sold, broken, hatched, corrupted, or used to power forbidden rituals.
- **Dragon scales and bone** fuel ancient magic, curses, relics, and weapons.
- **A living dragon** is the ultimate symbol of dominance—or the ultimate rival to eliminate.
- **Dragon sanctuaries** threaten the Pirate Kings because they restore balance outside King control.

Most of the **Five Pirate Kings** hunt dragons relentlessly. They raid volcanic islands for eggs, ambush migrating dragons at sea, and pay bounties for any captain who delivers a dragon—dead or alive—to their strongholds. The few dragons that remain are scattered, hidden, or captured.

### Your Mission: Rescue and Save the Dragons

You are not like the Kings. Your calling is to **rescue and save** the last of the great beasts—to find dragon eggs before hunters do, to free captured dragons from King strongholds, and to ferry them to **sanctuaries** where they can hatch and thrive in peace.

At first, this mission may seem like mercy.

Over time, the player learns it is also survival.

The dragons are not separate from the fate of the world. Saving them may be the only way to prevent a Second Drowning.

This puts you in **direct opposition** to most of the Pirate Kings. They will hunt you as fiercely as they hunt dragons—unless you outsmart them, outfight them, or find unlikely allies among those who do not share their obsession.

### Dragon Sanctuaries

Hidden islands and secret coves serve as **dragon sanctuaries**—places where rescued eggs can hatch and young dragons can grow without fear. Building, protecting, and supplying these sanctuaries is a core part of your mission.

The oldest sanctuaries may be protected by the dreams of Aurelion Veyr. Others are maintained by the **Tidewardens**, a secret network of lighthouse keepers, navigators, smugglers, healers, ex-Admiralty officers, old dragon priests, and sympathetic captains who move eggs under false cargo manifests.

Some say the Ghost Captain himself has been known to aid those who share this cause.

### Known Dragons of the Shattered Seas

The few dragons that remain are scattered across the seas. Each breed carries distinct traits—and each is hunted by the Kings who covet their power.

| Dragon | Breed / Trait | Where Found |
|--------|---------------|-------------|
| **Blackfang** | Fire • Dragonfire Spirit • Bonded Companion | Raised by Ebon Flameheart. The only dragon he allows to live. Their bond made Ebon nearly immortal. |
| **Blaze** | Fire • Volcanic | Volcanic islands of the Ashen Reach. Eggs nest in lava vents. |
| **Icey** | Frost • Northern | Frozen straits and iceberg caves. Rarely seen south of the ice line. |
| **Speedy** | Wind • Swift | Coastal cliffs and storm-wracked isles. Fastest of the great beasts. |
| **Elder** | Ancient • Wisdom | The oldest breed. Nest in ruins, hidden sanctuaries, and sunken temples. Nearly extinct. |
| **Aurelion Veyr** | Elder • Dawn • Oath | The Last Dawn-Wing. Saviour of Jasper's soul. His fate is unknown. |

**Young dragons**—hatchlings rescued from eggs or freed from captivity—grow into these forms. Baby Blaze, Baby Icey, Baby Speedy, and Baby Elder are among those you might save and ferry to sanctuary. Each one you rescue is a blow against the Kings who would see them destroyed.

### 1. Jasper Barrow — The Ghost Captain of the Righteous Tide

**Theme:** Curse • Justice • Ghosts • Vengeance  
**Alignment:** Chaotic Good (Relentless, Unforgiving)  
**Status:** Undead (Revenant / Reborn)  
**Dragon Stance:** protects  
**Image:** Images/PirateKings/JasperBarrow.png  
**Familiar Image:** Images/Familiars/Gloomfeather.png  
**Mythic Role:** The Witness • The First Drowned Oath • The Player's First Guide into Magic

**Backstory**  
Once the youngest son of Admiral Cassian Barrow, Jasper was raised on honour, duty, and the belief that the Admiralty existed to protect the innocent. Though denied command for years by court politics and his father's shadow, he eventually earned his own ship, **The Righteous Tide**, and became known as a captain who protected small ports, civilian convoys, and weak crews rather than chasing conquest.

His downfall came when he uncovered his father's betrayal. Cassian Barrow, Lord-Cartographer of the Admiralty, had been selling sacred dragon nest charts to pirate lords on behalf of the Atlantean Pearl Synod. Those pirate lords raided dragon nests across the world and sold the eggs into the deep to fuel the Crown Below ritual.

Jasper confronted his father and threatened to expose the conspiracy. Cassian responded by framing him as the traitor. Jasper was lured into a storm by a false distress call and ambushed by pirate lords carrying Atlantean weapons and Barrow charts. His ship was sunk. His crew was slaughtered. His name was ruined.

As Jasper drowned, the Elder Dragon **Aurelion Veyr** sensed the depth of the betrayal and the purity of Jasper's soul. Aurelion dived into the storm sea and preserved Jasper's spirit. When the Crown Below ritual shattered the world, Aurelion bound Jasper's soul to the sea with dragonfire and oath-magic.

Death could not still his sense of justice.

Now, Jasper hunts tyrants, pirate kings, corrupt rulers, and bullies—especially those who prey on weaker crews, islands, or dragons.

**Public Reputation**  
To many sailors, Jasper is a curse in the fog. Merchants fear him because he sinks corrupt convoys. Pirates fear him because he appears when they prey on the weak. Admiralty officers hate him because he attacks ships carrying sealed orders from corrupt old bloodlines. Common sailors leave lanterns in fog to beg safe passage.

Dragon sanctuary keepers and Tidewardens call him **The Tidebound Saint**, though Jasper despises the title.

**Dragons:** Jasper does **not** hunt dragons. He sees them as fellow victims of tyranny—hunted, betrayed, driven to the brink by the same conspiracy that destroyed his crew and broke the world. He has been known to aid captains who rescue dragons, guide lost eggs to sanctuary, and punish those who sell dragonkind for profit.

**Relationship to the Player**  
Jasper should be the player's first direct contact with the supernatural. At first, he appears as a terrifying rumour: a ghost captain who judges ships in fog. Then he becomes a rescuer, a test, a reluctant mentor, and eventually the emotional key to the true history of the Shattered Seas.

Jasper can punish corruption.

The player can heal what corruption broke.

That distinction should define their relationship.

**Familiar:** *Gloomfeather* — Spectral Parrot  
- Debuffs enemies with fear, misfortune, and cursed luck
- Scouts invisibly through fog and darkness
- Mimics enemy voices to sow panic
- Repeats fragments of Jasper's lost memories at key lore sites

**Domain:** *The Veilwake Sea* — fog-choked waters where ghost ships drift silently  
- His ghost fleet can ignore blockades
- Ports under his watch see reduced piracy… unless they're corrupt
- Broken oaths, hidden crimes, and blood-stained cargo may draw his attention
- Veilflame appears around wrecks connected to betrayal or dragon hunts

**Player Hooks**  
- Appears to aid bullied or outmatched players mid-fight
- May spare or reward honorable crews
- Tests whether the player protects the weak or exploits them
- Guides rescued dragon eggs through impossible fog routes
- Can become a powerful ally… or a relentless enemy

**Suggested First Encounter: "Ghost Lights in the Fog"**  
The player is attacked early by a stronger pirate ship on what should be a safe route. As defeat nears, fog rolls in. Green-gold lights appear on the water. The enemy's cannon fire dies in the air. A spectral warship emerges.

Jasper cripples the attackers, boards as an apparition, and studies the player. If the player carries an eggshell fragment, dragon-scale charm, or suspicious cargo, he says:

> "You carry a piece of a crime older than your blood."

Then he vanishes.

### 2. Captain Mordekai Drakon — The Sea Serpent King

**Theme:** Curse • Serpents • Ancient Magic  
**Alignment:** Lawful Evil  
**Status:** Cursed / Long-Lived  
**Dragon Stance:** hunts  
**Image:** Images/PirateKings/Captain_Mordekai_Drakon.png  
**Familiar Image:** Images/Familiars/Ssyrix.png  
**Mythic Role:** The Serpent Heir • The Wound of Atlantean Cruelty • The Rival Claimant to Draconic Power

**Backstory**  
Mordekai Drakon was not born a king. He was a frightened boy aboard his father's merchant ship during the violent decline after the Unbinding Tide.

His father was one of the few remaining merchant captains brave enough to sail between free human ports while Atlantean remnants, sea serpents, krakens, pirates, and storm routes tore the world apart. He carried goods, messages, refugees, and forbidden charts through waters that no sane navy would patrol.

Before one dangerous voyage, Mordekai's father gave him a strange heirloom: a **magic skull dagger** carved from black bone and old silver. He told Mordekai it was not a toy, not a treasure, but a last promise.

> "If the sea takes everything else, keep this close."

The Atlanteans ambushed them.

Whether the ship carried contraband, rebel supplies, unpaid tribute, or simply refused to kneel is a matter of rumour. What matters is that Atlantean raiders burned the sails, shattered the hull, and killed Mordekai's father in front of him.

Mordekai survived by clinging to a floating barrel amid wreckage, bodies, cargo, and fire.

Then the sea opened beneath him.

A colossal sea serpent, one of the great unbound beasts empowered by the Crown Below's corruption, swallowed him whole.

Inside the serpent, in darkness and acid, Mordekai should have died. Instead, terrified and half-mad, he drew his father's skull dagger and stabbed until he found the serpent's heart.

The dagger pierced it.

The serpent died.

Mordekai did not.

The dagger, the serpent's heart, Mordekai's grief, and the corrupted magic of the deep fused together. The serpent's dying spirit, **Ssyrix**, bound itself to the boy who had killed it from within.

Mordekai emerged from the corpse changed.

The frightened child became something colder.

The sea had taken his father, his ship, his childhood, and his mercy. In return, it gave him power.

Now Mordekai rules through fear and inevitability. He hates Atlanteans for what they did to his family, but he has become a tyrant in their image: convinced that safety comes only through domination.

**Dragons:** Mordekai **hunts dragons** relentlessly. Serpents and dragons are ancient rivals—both draconic, both claiming dominion over the world's primal forces. Ssyrix whispers that dragons stole the world's reverence while serpents were driven into the deep. Mordekai consumes dragon scales and bone to feed Ssyrix, strengthen his curse, and extend his own power.

His lieutenants raid volcanic islands, ambush migrating dragons at sea, and seek old nest charts from the age of Cassian Barrow.

**Familiar:** *Ssyrix* — The Bound Serpent Spirit  
- Wraps ships, immobilizing them
- Inflicts stacking curses over time
- Can briefly possess Mordekai to amplify power
- Whispers ancient anti-dragon lore
- Hungers for dragonbone and Atlantean relics alike

**Domain:** *The Coiled Expanse* — waters marked by massive serpentine shapes beneath the waves  
- Ships move slower
- Crew morale drains over time
- Serpent wakes can redirect routes
- Cursed offers appear after major victories
- Atlantean wrecks and serpent shrines lie half-swallowed beneath the currents

**Player Hooks**  
- Hunts players who grow too powerful too quickly
- May offer cursed power in exchange for loyalty
- Tempts the player to trade dragon eggs for ship upgrades or serpent boons
- Can provide anti-Atlantean lore that is useful but poisoned by hatred
- Slaying him risks releasing Ssyrix upon the world

### 3. Lady Adara Thalassa — Queen of the Leviathans

**Theme:** Atlantis • Leviathans • Ocean Sovereignty  
**Alignment:** Neutral (Ruthlessly Pragmatic)  
**Status:** Atlantean Heir / Living Sovereign  
**Dragon Stance:** hunts  
**Image:** Images/PirateKings/Lady_Adara_Thalassa.png  
**Familiar Image:** Images/Familiars/Pearl.png  
**Mythic Role:** The Heir of the Guilty Empire • The Temptation of Order • The Last Dream of the Drowned Crown

**Backstory**  
Adara was born to a sunken empire—one of the last true descendants of Atlantis. She grew up among coral ruins, drowned courts, broken tide-engines, and murals that told a polished version of the past: Atlantis as saviour, the surface as betrayer, dragons as proud beasts who refused necessary sacrifice, and the Age of the Drowned Crown as the world's lost golden order.

Where surface sailors see tribute, Adara sees protection.

Where free captains see tyranny, she sees law.

Where Tidewardens see the sin of the Crown Below, she sees a flawed ritual that could have worked if guided by stronger hands.

When her people fell into factional ruin, Adara reclaimed their legacy by stealing the **Trident of the Tides**, an artifact granting command over leviathans.

She does not raid for gold. She conquers to rebuild a lost civilization beneath the sea. Islands that swear fealty are protected. Those that resist are erased.

Adara does not think of herself as a pirate.

She considers the surface ports illegal squatters on Atlantean waters.

**Second Age Doctrine**  
Adara's dream is not merely to restore Atlantis as it was before the Shattering. She wants to restore the authority of the **Drowned Crown**: the tribute routes, current engines, leviathan roads, and sea-law that once made the Second Age possible.

In her mind, the Unbinding Tide did not prove that Atlantis was wrong to rule.

It proved Atlantis was not ruthless enough.

**Dragons:** Adara **hunts dragons** as obstacles to her ocean sovereignty. Dragons are ancient, powerful, and refuse to bow—they threaten her claim to rule the seas. She captures eggs for Atlantean rituals and slays adult dragons that encroach on her domain. She believes the Crown Below failed because it was corrupted, rushed, or betrayed—not because Atlantis was wrong to attempt it.

Delivering a dragon egg to her stronghold earns rare favor—and damns the dragon.

**Familiar:** *Pearl* — Juvenile Leviathan  
- Shields allies with tidal barriers
- Heals ships and structures
- Grows stronger the longer Adara reigns
- Can sense Atlantean relics and Crown Below fragments

**Domain:** *The Drowned Crown* — a semi-submerged kingdom of coral and ruins  
- Can flood islands
- Controls trade routes via sea beasts
- Offers protection to ports that swear fealty
- Houses murals, archives, and ritual chambers tied to the true history of the Crown Below
- Contains broken infrastructure from the Age of the Drowned Crown

**Player Hooks**  
- Can become a powerful political ally
- Might task players with reclaiming Atlantean relics
- Offers lawful safety at the cost of freedom
- Presents the strongest argument that tyranny can look like order
- Open war with her reshapes the map

### 4. Nimue Tideborn — The Kraken Caller

**Alignment:** Chaotic Neutral  
**Status:** Kraken-Bonded / Time-Touched  
**Dragon Stance:** hunts  
**Image:** Images/PirateKings/Nimue_Tideborn.png  
**Familiar Image:** Images/Familiars/Inkshadow.png  
**Mythic Role:** The Listener at the Wound • The Human Doorway into the Deep • Child of the Unbinding Tide

**Backstory**  
Nimue Tideborn was once a fisher's daughter obsessed with legends of the Unbinding Tide. While other children were warned not to sing near black water, Nimue memorised the forbidden verses. She collected broken shell tablets, abyssal stones, drowned bells, and scraps of Atlantean warnings about krakens that had broken free from old commands.

One day, she discovered an abyssal song etched into black stone.

The song was not Atlantean.

It was older, deeper, and hungry.

When Nimue sang it, she did not summon death.

She summoned a young Kraken.

Instead of fleeing, she bonded with it.

Unlike the Atlanteans, Nimue did not command the creature through tide-song. She listened. She fed it. She answered it. She let its dreams into her sleep and its hunger into her heart.

As the Kraken grew, so did Nimue's power—and her distance from humanity. The creature became the strongest Kraken in the ocean and, by the current age, something ancient in all but origin. Nimue no longer raids ports in any ordinary sense. She drags entire fleets into the dark to "feed the deep."

Nimue's song may be older than Atlantis. Some scholars believe the failed Crown Below ritual woke something beneath the world, and that her Kraken is not merely a beast but a child, limb, or messenger of that deeper presence.

Nimue may not be conquering the sea.

She may be listening to it scream.

**Dragons:** Nimue **hunts dragons** to feed the Kraken. Dragons are powerful prey—their essence sustains the deep and empowers her bond with the abyss. She sends her servants to lure dragons into the Black Spiral, where the Kraken drags them under. A dragon egg is a rare delicacy; a full-grown dragon, a feast.

**Familiar:** *Inkshadow* — Living Shadow Octopus

- Can merge with the Kraken to empower it

**Domain:** *The Black Spiral* — waters that absorb light and sound  
- Visibility is severely reduced
- Sanity-testing environment
- Compass drift and false audio cues
- Ancient lore can sometimes resolve encounters that combat cannot
- Kraken signs appear before the creature itself: silence, missing stars, water that swallows lantern light

**Player Hooks**  
- May ignore players entirely… until provoked
- Can be reasoned with via ancient lore
- May reveal hints about what woke beneath the Crown Below
- Represents the freedom of the deep becoming horror rather than justice
- Killing the Kraken may break her mind—or free her

### 5. Captain Ebon Flameheart — The Dragon Marauder

**Theme:** Fire • Dragons • Conquest  
**Alignment:** Chaotic Evil  
**Status:** Dragonfire-Blessed / Nearly Immortal  
**Dragon Stance:** hunts  
**Image:** Images/PirateKings/Flameheart_01.png  
**Familiar Image:** Images/Familiars/Ember.png  
**Mythic Role:** The Glory of Men • The Ashen Turn • The False Dragon King

**Backstory**  
Ebon Flameheart was not always the nightmare whispered of in burned ports.

In the early Third Age, he was a legend of the Free Sails: a pirate captain, monster-hunter, route-breaker, teacher, and symbol of human defiance after the collapse of Atlantean rule. He broke serpent blockades, fought kraken-touched raiders, charted ash currents, opened dangerous trade lanes, and taught free captains how to survive in a world of monsters.

Pirates still sing that Ebon gave glory back to men.

Merchants still admit that some routes exist because he opened them.

Even his enemies cannot deny that he helped create the Age of Free Sails.

But Ebon's glory carried a hidden cost. He did not only teach captains how to fight monsters. He taught them how to hunt dragons.

He made dragon-hunting repeatable.

Profitable.

Teachable.

He turned the old sin of stolen eggs into an industry.

During a raid on a volcanic rookery, Ebon found a surviving dragon egg among ash and broken shell. His crew expected him to sell it. Instead, he kept it. The hatchling that emerged was **Blackfang**.

For a time, Ebon and Blackfang were friends. Ebon raised him from a baby aboard his ship. Blackfang became companion, weapon, mascot, terror, and miracle. Their bond was not born from chains, but from shared fire, battle, hunger, and survival.

That bond blessed Ebon with **dragonfire spirit**. His wounds burned closed. His blood smoked. Flame could scar him but not easily kill him. His age slowed until sailors began whispering that Flameheart would never die.

Then friendship became fear.

Ebon realised that if Blackfang had chosen him, another dragon might choose another captain. Another pirate might gain dragonfire spirit. Another crew might become legend. Another Dragon King might rise.

So he turned on the very possibility.

Now Ebon burns dragon nests, slaughters migrating dragons, and pays bounties for eggs so he can destroy them himself. He does not want every dragon dead because he hates Blackfang.

He wants every other dragon dead because Blackfang made him powerful.

There will be only one Dragon King.

**Dragons:** Ebon **hunts dragons** to eliminate all rivals. He has Blackfang—the only dragon that matters to him. Every other dragon is a threat: another captain could hatch an egg, another King could claim a beast, another free dragon could challenge his myth.

Ebon's dragon hunts are not random cruelty. They are strategic extinction.

**Blackfang:** Blackfang is more than a pet or weapon. He was once Ebon's chosen companion. Their bond is real, which makes Ebon's corruption more tragic and more dangerous. Blackfang may still love Ebon, fear him, resent him, or be unable to separate his own rage from Ebon's dragonfire spirit.

If Ebon falls, Blackfang may become unbound—not as a simple boss reward, but as a living consequence.

**Familiar:** *Ember* — Fire Phoenix  
- Revives after death
- Ignites ships and crews
- Buffs allies with flame-based rage
- Marks targets for Blackfang's fire

**Domain:** *The Ashen Reach* — scorched islands and burning seas  
- Constant fire hazards
- Volcanic events during combat
- Refugee ports and burned markets
- Dragon hunter bounties are common
- Old Free Sail monuments now stand blackened beneath Ebon's banners

**Player Hooks**  
- Ultimate test of naval combat skill
- Can trigger massive world events if unchecked
- Offers huge rewards for dragon eggs, then destroys them
- His old teachings may appear in early tutorial content, creating a delayed villain reveal
- Slaying him may leave Blackfang unbound… and furious

## The Archipelago & Your Place in It

### Home Waters

The **inner archipelago**—where you begin—is the last bastion of order. Ports here pay lip service to distant crowns, but in practice they answer to merchants, guilds, lighthouse keepers, and whoever keeps the peace. This is where new captains earn their stripes: trading goods, taking contracts, hiring crew, and building infamy.

The Home Waters should feel bright, readable, and adventurous at first. The deeper lore appears as fragments: old dragon symbols on cargo crates, tavern rumours about ghost lights, a lighthouse keeper who refuses to sail through fog, a faded quote from one of Ebon's old sailing manuals, or a merchant who panics when the player finds an eggshell fragment.

Possible Home Waters locations:

- **Port Mercy** — starter port, market, shipwright, and basic contracts.
- **Lantern Cay** — lighthouse island and rumour hub.
- **Saltmarket** — trade hub with competing merchant guilds.
- **The Old Rookery** — abandoned dragon nesting cliffs and possible first egg discovery.
- **Greywake Shoals** — early wreck salvage site tied to Jasper's false treason.
- **Free Sail Yard** — training docks where Ebon's old route lessons survive as tutorial material.

### The Outer Seas

Beyond the safe routes, the map grows strange. **Route modifiers** reflect the influence of the Kings:

- **Fog** — Veilwake Sea (Jasper's domain)
- **Serpent marks** — Coiled Expanse (Mordekai)
- **Coral ruins, tidal currents** — Drowned Crown (Adara)
- **Darkness, abyssal waters** — Black Spiral (Nimue)
- **Ash, volcanic vents** — Ashen Reach (Ebon)

The Outer Seas are not only more dangerous. They are closer to the wound left by the Crown Below. Routes shift, compasses lie, old charts become unreliable, and the supernatural becomes harder to dismiss.

### Pirate King Strongholds

Each King maintains **strongholds** in their domain. Defeating a **Lieutenant**—a trusted captain who serves a King—reveals the coordinates of that King's stronghold. Raiding a stronghold is the ultimate challenge: unique loot, legendary encounters, and—for those who dare—**rescued dragons** held in captivity.

Strongholds should also reveal lore. Each one contains a different piece of the truth:

- Jasper's domain reveals betrayal, oath, and lost Admiralty records.
- Mordekai's domain reveals Atlantean cruelty, serpent myths, and cursed survival.
- Adara's domain reveals Atlantean murals, Second Age doctrine, Crown Below fragments, and Pearl Synod propaganda.
- Nimue's domain reveals abyssal songs, kraken memory, and hints of what woke beneath the waves.
- Ebon's domain reveals the glory and horror of the Age of Free Sails, Blackfang's bond, and the industrialisation of dragon hunting.

### Dragon Sanctuaries

Hidden islands and secret coves serve as **dragon sanctuaries**—places where rescued eggs can hatch and young dragons can grow in peace. Finding, building, and protecting these sanctuaries is central to your mission. Some lie in the Home Waters; others require venturing into the outer seas.

The sanctuaries are supported by the **Tidewardens**, a secret network of lighthouse keepers, smugglers, healers, old navigators, ex-Admiralty officers, and dragon-sympathizers.

Their motto:

> **"No crown owns the sky. No king owns the sea."**

### The Tidewardens

The Tidewardens are not a formal nation. They are a hidden rescue network. They move dragon eggs under false cargo manifests, mark safe routes with lighthouse codes, shelter hunted crews, and preserve fragments of the true history.

They are ideal quest-givers for:

- dragon egg delivery contracts,
- sanctuary supply runs,
- rescue missions,
- hidden route unlocks,
- lore hunts,
- reputation rewards.

Some Tidewardens revere Jasper. Others fear him. All know that when the fog glows green-gold, someone nearby is being judged.

## Tone & Narrative Beats

**Tone:** Adventurous, slightly comedic, readable fantasy piracy—with darker undertones when the Kings are involved.

The world should feel like **fun pirate adventure on the surface, wounded myth underneath**.

The player should be able to enjoy tavern rumours, cargo runs, eccentric crew traits, ship upgrades, broadside combat, and boss fights without needing a lore lecture. But the farther they sail, the more they discover that the economy, monsters, dragons, and Pirate Kings are all symptoms of the same ancient wound.

### Your Story

You are a new captain climbing the ranks in the Home Waters. At first, your goals are practical: make profit, hire crew, upgrade your ship, and survive.

Then you encounter something that should not exist.

Ghost lights in the fog.

A dragon egg hidden in ordinary cargo.

A chart marked with a symbol older than the Admiralty.

A spectral captain who knows your cargo before you do.

From there, your secret mission begins: **rescue and save the last of the dragons**. The Five Pirate Kings hunt them relentlessly; you defy them by finding eggs before hunters do, freeing captured dragons from strongholds, and ferrying them to sanctuaries where they can thrive.

The Shattered Seas offer profit, danger, and legend—but your true calling is to protect what the Kings would destroy.

Defeat lieutenants, uncover stronghold locations, recover the truth of the Crown Below, and decide: will you serve a King, defy them, or carve your own path—and save the dragons before the world drowns again?

## Player-Facing Lore Reveal Structure

The truth of the Shattered Seas should unfold in layers rather than exposition dumps.

### Layer 1 — Rumour

The player hears contradictory explanations for the Shattering, the Drowned Crown, the Unbinding Tide, the Free Sails, and the Pirate Kings in taverns, markets, ports, and crew chatter.

Example lines:

- "My gran said the sea rose in one night. Took three kingdoms before breakfast."
- "Was dragons, I heard. Wings of fire from horizon to horizon."
- "Nah. Atlantis did it. Dug too deep, sang too loud."
- "Don't say Barrow's name in fog. He hears."
- "Flameheart? Say what you like, but before him no merchant crossed the Ashen Reach alive."
- "The Unbinding Tide? That's when the sea bit through its leash."

### Layer 2 — Evidence

The player finds physical clues:

- old Admiralty charts with dragon nest markings,
- pirate ledgers listing "warm pearls" or "sky eggs,"
- Atlantean coin in surface wrecks,
- eggshell fragments inside ritual crates,
- lighthouse records showing Jasper was framed,
- a broken Barrow family seal on a pirate contract,
- shattered tide-gate mechanisms from the Second Age,
- serpent-scarred coral roads from the Unbinding Tide,
- old Free Sail manuals credited to Ebon Flameheart,
- dragonbone tools that prove Ebon's methods spread across pirate culture.

### Layer 3 — Jasper's Memory

Jasper appears after key discoveries. He does not explain everything immediately. He reacts emotionally.

At first:

> "That mark. I watched men die beneath that seal."

Later:

> "Those charts were never stolen. They were given."

Finally:

> "My father sold the sky to the sea, and called it order."

On Ebon:

> "Flameheart opened routes, aye. He paved them with wings."

### Layer 4 — Faction Testimony

Each major faction tells a different part of the truth:

- **Adara's Atlanteans** reveal the grandeur and propaganda of the Drowned Crown.
- **Mordekai's serpent cults** reveal Atlantean cruelty and the trauma of survival.
- **Nimue's abyssal lore** reveals the Unbinding Tide from the perspective of the deep.
- **Ebon's old manuals and burned ports** reveal the Age of Free Sails and its cost.
- **The Tidewardens** reveal the dragon rescue network and the hidden continuity of the First Age.

### Layer 5 — The Crown Below

Late in the campaign, the player sees the ruined engine itself or a projection of it. Only then does the full myth become undeniable: the world was not broken by accident, and the same forces could break it again.

## Suggested Jasper Questline

### Quest 1: Ghost Lights in the Fog

The player is saved by Jasper during an unfair pirate ambush.

**Purpose:** Introduce magic, ghost ships, moral judgment, and Jasper as terrifying protector rather than simple villain.

### Quest 2: The Traitor's Bell

A port asks the player to recover a bell from a wreck believed to belong to Jasper's "traitor ship." The bell is from **The Righteous Tide**. When returned, it rings by itself in fog.

**Purpose:** Establish that Jasper's public reputation may be false.

### Quest 3: Charts Written in Blood

The player finds an old Admiralty chart showing dragon nests. It bears Cassian Barrow's seal. Jasper becomes hostile at first, believing the player may be another egg-runner.

**Purpose:** Reveal Cassian's betrayal and connect dragon hunts to the Admiralty.

### Quest 4: The Last Clutch of Emberfall

The player rescues their first dragon egg from pirates using one of Cassian's old charts. Jasper guides the player through fog to a hidden sanctuary route.

**Purpose:** Make the player a true Sanctuary Runner.

### Quest 5: The Father's Name

The player uncovers evidence that Cassian Barrow framed Jasper.

Possible objectives:

- find Admiralty court records,
- intercept a sealed naval archive,
- recover a pirate confession,
- explore a ruined Barrow estate.

**Purpose:** Clear Jasper's name emotionally, even if the world never fully believes it.

### Quest 6: Aurelion's Dive

The player discovers the memory of the Elder Dragon who saved Jasper. This could happen through a dragon sanctuary, a dream sequence, or a golden scale that reacts to Jasper's Veilflame.

**Purpose:** Reveal that Jasper was not cursed by evil magic; he was bound by a dragon oath.

### Quest 7: The Crown Below

The player learns that the ritual still pulses beneath the world. Jasper asks the player not only to avenge the past, but to prevent its repetition.

**Purpose:** Turn the campaign from "rescue dragons" into "stop the Second Drowning."

## Suggested Historical Reveal Questline: The Ages of the Sea

### Quest 1: The Pearl Tax Ledger

The player recovers a ruined ledger from a drowned tollhouse showing how Atlanteans taxed surface ports during the Age of the Drowned Crown.

**Purpose:** Introduce the Second Age as a period of Atlantean dominance after the Shattering.

### Quest 2: The Broken Tide Gate

The player explores a shattered Atlantean tide gate wrapped in fossilised serpent marks.

**Purpose:** Reveal that serpents and krakens rebelled against Atlantean control.

### Quest 3: The Unbinding Song

The player hears fragments of an abyssal song connected to Nimue's Kraken.

**Purpose:** Connect the Unbinding Tide to Nimue and the Black Spiral.

### Quest 4: Free Sail Lessons

The player finds one of Ebon's old instructional charts teaching captains how to avoid monster routes.

**Purpose:** Present Ebon first as a legendary teacher and pirate hero.

### Quest 5: Ash on the Rookery

The player discovers that the same techniques in Ebon's manuals were later used to raid dragon nests.

**Purpose:** Reframe the Age of Free Sails as both prosperity and dragon-hunting industrialisation.

### Quest 6: Blackfang's First Chain

The player discovers evidence that Blackfang was raised by Ebon from a hatchling, and that their bond was once genuine.

**Purpose:** Add tragedy and complexity to Ebon's villainy.

### Quest 7: The Ashen Turn

The player uncovers the moment Ebon began destroying dragon eggs to prevent anyone else from gaining dragonfire spirit.

**Purpose:** Establish Ebon as the current age's great dragon-hoarding tyrant.

## Appendix: Internal Truth Summary

This is the true version of the myth for writer/designer reference:

> In the First Age, humans of the land, Atlanteans of the sea, and dragons of the sky kept the world in balance. The Atlanteans, resentful of their dependence on surface maps and dragon migration, built the Crown Below, a ritual engine meant to bind all realms under ocean rule.
>
> Because Atlanteans could not walk the land, they bribed Admiral Cassian Barrow, keeper of the Admiralty's forbidden dragon charts. Cassian gave those charts to pirate lords, who raided nests across the world and sold the eggs into the deep.
>
> Jasper Barrow discovered his father's betrayal and confronted him. Rather than repent, Cassian framed his son as the traitor and lured him into a storm, where pirate lords sank his ship.
>
> As Jasper drowned, an Elder Dragon named Aurelion Veyr sensed both the betrayal and the purity of Jasper's soul. Aurelion dived into the sea and preserved Jasper's spirit.
>
> When the Atlanteans performed the Crown Below ritual, the stolen dragon eggs shattered the balance of land, sea, and sky. Atlantis drowned, the continents broke, the dragons vanished, and the Shattered Seas were born.
>
> In the chaos, Aurelion bound Jasper's soul to the sea as a revenant guardian: a ghost captain sworn to protect the innocent, punish corruption, and guide the last dragons to sanctuary.
>
> After the Shattering, Atlantis dominated the flooded world during the Age of the Drowned Crown, but the Crown Below's corruption strengthened sea serpents and krakens until the Unbinding Tide destroyed Atlantean infrastructure.
>
> In the power vacuum, the Age of Free Sails began. Ebon Flameheart brought glory to pirates and helped reopen trade, but he also taught the world how to hunt dragons. After raising Blackfang and gaining dragonfire spirit, he became nearly immortal and began destroying all other dragons to prevent any rival from gaining the same power.
>
> The current age is the Age of Five Crowns. The Pirate Kings divide the seas. The dragons are nearly extinct. The Crown Below still pulses.
>
> The world remembers only rumours.
>
> Jasper remembers the truth.
